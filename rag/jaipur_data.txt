2.1 AMER FORT (Amber Fort)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Location          Devisinghpura, Amer, Jaipur – 11 km from city center
Timings           8:00 AM – 5:30 PM (last entry 5:00 PM)
Entry Fee 2026    ₹100 Indian / ₹500 Foreigner (₹200/₹550 with audio guide)
Best Time         8:00–10:00 AM (avoid crowds & heat); October–March
Time Needed       2.5–3 hours

New 2026 Experiences
• Shikara Boat Ride: Starting April 2026 at Maota Lake – ₹1000/30 min, luxury electric
  boats with peacock design.
• Light & Sound Show: English 7:00 PM, Hindi 8:00 PM (March-April timings).
• Elephant Ride: ₹2500 for 2 people, morning slots 7:00–10:30 AM (3 rounds).

Description       UNESCO World Heritage site blending Mughal & Rajput architecture.
                  Four courtyards include Sheesh Mahal (Mirror Palace) with thousands
                  of tiny mirrors. Built in 1592 by Raja Man Singh I.

Insider Tips      • Buy composite ticket (₹300 Indian/₹1000 Foreigner) for multiple
                    monuments.
                  • Jeep ride from base: ₹400 for up to 5 people.
                  • Best photos: Maota Lake reflection at sunrise.

Connected Site    2 km underground tunnel to Jaigarh Fort (currently closed for safety).

2.2 JAIGARH FORT (Military Heritage)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Location          Cheel ka Teela, Aravalli Hills – 15 km from Jaipur
Timings           9:00 AM – 4:30 PM (last entry 4:00 PM)
Entry Fee 2026    ₹35 Indian / ₹85 Foreigner
Best Time         9:00–11:00 AM for photography; October–March
Time Needed       1.5–2 hours

Star Attraction   Jaivana Cannon – World's largest wheeled cannon: 20.19 ft long,
                  50 tons, 11-inch diameter, range 35 km.

Unique Features   • Only surviving cannon foundry in India (Dungar workshop).
                  • Secret 2 km tunnel to Amber Fort.
                  • Sagar Lake – one of India's largest water tanks.
                  • Laxmi Vilas Palace with murals & mirror work.

Insider Tips      • Never conquered in battle – "Victory Fort" nickname authentic.
                  • Combine with Amber Fort (2 km apart) – shared jeeps available ₹50-100.
                  • Wear sturdy shoes for uneven surfaces.

2.3 HAWA MAHAL (Palace of Winds)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Location          Jaipur city center
Timings           9:00 AM – 4:30 PM
Entry Fee         ₹50 Indian / ₹200 Foreigner (composite ticket available)
Best Time         Early morning for photography (golden light on pink sandstone)

Current 2026      Special Coin Exhibition (March 17–19, 2026): 154 ancient coins from
Event             gold, silver, copper – featuring 16th-century Shah Jahan coin,
                  500g weight, 13.4 cm diameter. Exhibition in first courtyard gallery.

Description       953 windows (jharokhas) designed for royal women to observe street
                  life unseen. Built 1799 by Maharaja Sawai Pratap Singh.

Insider Tips      • Best viewed from outside – photos from street opposite.
                  • Visit during Rajasthan Diwas celebrations for folk performances.
                  • 15 display cases with artifacts from Panch Mark to Mughal periods.

2.4 CITY PALACE JAIPUR
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Location          Old City, Jaipur
Timings           9:30 AM – 5:00 PM
Entry Fee         Part of composite ticket (₹300 Indian/₹1000 Foreigner for 2 days)
Best Time         Morning to avoid crowds

Description       Still residence of Jaipur royal family. Includes Mubarak Mahal,
                  Chandra Mahal, museum with textiles & armory. Privy Purse Room
                  displays silver vessels weighing 340 kg – Guinness World Record.

Insider Tips      • Buy composite ticket covering Amer, Jaigarh, Nahargarh, Hawa Mahal.
                  • Royal family members sometimes visible in private sections.
                  • Photography not allowed inside museum buildings.

2.5 NAHARGARH FORT
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Location          Aravalli hills – 15 km from Jaipur
Timings           10:00 AM – 6:00 PM
Best Time         Sunset (panoramic Jaipur city views)
Entry Fee         ₹50 Indian / ₹200 Foreigner (in composite ticket)

Description       Built in 1734 by Sawai Jai Singh II. Originally "Abode of Tigers,"
                  named after Prince Nahar. Madhavendra Bhawan has 12 identical suites
                  for queens.

Insider Tips      • Best sunset photography in Jaipur.
                  • Combine with Jaigarh & Amer for full fort circuit.

Nearby            Nahargarh Biological Park – wildlife safari with Asiatic lions,
                  tigers, panthers (₹50 park entry + safari charges).

2.6 JAL MAHAL
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Location          Man Sagar Lake, Amer Road
Timings           View anytime from roadside
Best Time         Sunset photography

2026 Update       Shikara Rides Available at Man Sagar Lake – luxury electric boats
                  with Rajasthani heritage theme operating. 30 min rides from ₹1000.

Description       18th-century palace appearing to float on water. Only top floor
                  visible when lake full.

Insider Tips      • No entry inside – view from road or boat.
                  • Best photo point: Jal Mahal Viewpoint opposite.

2.7 JANTAR MANTAR
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Location          Near City Palace
Timings           9:00 AM – 4:30 PM
Entry Fee         Included in composite ticket
Best Time         Morning for clear shadow readings

Description       UNESCO World Heritage site. Largest stone astronomical observatory
                  built by Sawai Jai Singh II in 1734. Features 19 instruments including
                  the world’s largest stone sundial (Samrat Yantra), accurate to 2 seconds.

Insider Tips      • Take a guide (₹300-500) to understand astronomical functions.
                  • Excellent for educational visits.

3. OFFBEAT & CULTURAL GEMS
================================================================================

3.1 PANNA MEENA KA KUND (Stepwell)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Location          Near Amer – 12 km from city center
Entry Fee         Free
Best Time         7:00–9:00 AM, especially winter

Description       16th-century symmetrical stepwell with criss-crossing staircases.
                  Community water source and gathering space. Popular for photography
                  but often less crowded.

Insider Tips      • Excellent photography geometry.
                  • Combine with Amer Fort visit.
                  • Quiet, contemplative atmosphere.

3.2 GAITORE KI CHHATRIYAN (Royal Cenotaphs)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Location          6 km from city center, quiet valley
Entry Fee         ₹50 Indian / ₹100 Foreigner
Best Time         Morning or late afternoon, October–March

Description       Kachwaha Rajput rulers' cremation ground. Intricately carved marble
                  cenotaphs blending Rajasthani & Mughal styles. Peaceful setting.

Insider Tips      • Most contemplative heritage site in Jaipur.
                  • Surrounded by hills & bird songs.

3.3 SISODIA RANI GARDEN
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Location          6 km from city center
Entry Fee         ₹50 Indian / ₹200 Foreigner
Best Time         Morning hours, winter & early spring

Description       Terraced garden built as romantic retreat. Indo-Persian & Rajasthani
                  architecture with Radha-Krishna murals. Charbagh (four-part) Mughal
                  garden style.

Insider Tips      • Far less crowded than other gardens.
                  • Near Mount Sumel trek start point.

3.4 ANOKHI MUSEUM OF HAND PRINTING
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Location          Haveli near Amer – 16 km from city center
Entry Fee         ₹80 Indian / ₹150 Foreigner
Best Time         Late morning to early afternoon
Closed            Mondays

Description       Restored haveli documenting Rajasthan's block-printing traditions.
                  UNESCO award for heritage conservation. Working artisans sometimes
                  demonstrating.

Insider Tips      • On-site shop with authentic hand-printed textiles.
                  • Allow 1-1.5 hours.
                  • Photography allowed in designated areas.

3.5 MOUNT SUMEL TREK (Adventure)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Location          Near Jamdoli & Sumel Village, Agra Highway (NH48) – 8-10 km from Jaipur
Entry Fee         Free
Trek Distance     2–3 km round trip
Duration          1–3 hours
Difficulty        Easy to moderate
Best Time         October–March; Monsoon (lush greenery); Avoid May–June heat

Description       Hidden trekking spot in Aravalli hills with panoramic views of
                  Kanota Dam and Jaipur skyline. Sunrise treks highly recommended.

Insider Tips      • Start before dawn for sunrise views.
                  • Carry 1-2 liters water, snacks, sunscreen.
                  • No formal signage – use Google Maps pin "Sumel Village".
                  • Combine with Sisodia Rani Garden.

3.6 CHAND BAORI, ABHANERI (Day Trip)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Location          95 km from Jaipur (1.5-2 hr drive)
Entry Fee         Free
Best Time         Morning hours, October–February

Description       One of world's deepest stepwells: 3,500 symmetrical steps, 13 levels
                  descending 100 feet. Built 8th-9th century. Featured in movies like
                  "The Dark Knight Rises."

Insider Tips      • Half-day excursion from Jaipur.
                  • Combine with Abhaneri village and Harshat Mata Temple (9th century).
                  • Best visited on way to/from Agra if traveling.

3.7 GALTA JI (Monkey Temple)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Location          Galta, Jaipur – 10 km from city center
Entry Fee         Free (small fee for camera)
Best Time         Early morning or late afternoon; avoid weekends for peace

Description       Ancient Hindu pilgrimage site set in a mountain pass. Natural springs
                  fill sacred tanks (kunds). Large population of resident monkeys.
                  Built around 18th century.

Insider Tips      • Sacred to Hanuman; temple complex has 7 tanks.
                  • Spectacular views of Jaipur from top.
                  • Watch your belongings – monkeys are cheeky.
                  • Photography restrictions inside main temple.

3.8 ALBERT HALL MUSEUM
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Location          Ram Niwas Garden, Jaipur
Timings           10:00 AM – 5:00 PM (closed Mondays)
Entry Fee         ₹40 Indian / ₹300 Foreigner
Best Time         Afternoon (cool interiors)

Description       Oldest museum in Rajasthan, built in 1876. Indo-Saracenic architecture.
                  Displays include miniature paintings, sculptures, metalwork,
                  international art, and Egyptian mummy.

Insider Tips      • Light show in evening (6:30 PM English, 7:30 PM Hindi).
                  • Ram Niwas Garden also houses zoo, art gallery, and planetarium.

10. SAFETY & PRACTICAL INFORMATION
================================================================================

HEALTH & SAFETY
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
• Drink bottled or filtered water; avoid tap water.
• Carry hand sanitizer and basic first-aid kit.
• Vaccinations recommended: Hepatitis A, Typhoid, Tetanus.
• Use sunscreen (SPF 50+) and hat, even in winter.
• For emergencies: Ambulance 108, Police 100, Fire 101.
• Hospitals: Sawai Man Singh (SMS) Hospital (government), Fortis, Eternal Heart Care.

LOCAL ETIQUETTE & CULTURE
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
• Dress modestly, especially at temples and rural areas – cover shoulders and knees.
• Remove shoes before entering temples and some heritage sites.
• Photography prohibited inside many temples and museums – look for signs.
• Right hand is traditionally used for giving/receiving money or food.
• Ask permission before photographing locals, especially rural or tribal individuals.
• Tipping is customary for guides, drivers, hotel staff – ₹50-200 or 10% at restaurants.

MONEY & COMMUNICATION
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
• ATMs widely available; carry cash for smaller shops and auto-rickshaws.
• Major credit cards accepted at mid-range to luxury establishments.
• SIM cards: Local prepaid SIM available with passport and photo; Jio and Airtel have best coverage.
• Wi-Fi available at hotels, cafes; data speed generally good in city.

LANGUAGE TIPS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
• Basic Hindi phrases: Namaste (hello), Dhanyavad (thank you), Kitne ka hai? (how much?),
  Bahut mahanga (too expensive) – useful for bargaining.

SPECIAL 2026 CONSIDERATIONS

PRO TRAVEL TIPS – THE ELITE EXPERIENCE
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
1. Sunrise/Sunset Photography: Panna Meena ka Kund (sunrise), Nahargarh (sunset),
   Jal Mahal (sunset), Hawa Mahal (early morning).
2. Heritage Walks: Join “Pink City Heritage Walk” (organized by Rajasthan Tourism)
   for guided exploration of old city lanes, havelis, and markets.
3. Private Tours: For exclusive experiences, book “Palace on Wheels” (luxury train)
   or private heritage walks with documented guides (approved by Department of Tourism).
4. Local Experiences: Attend a block-printing workshop (Anokhi Museum, Sanganer),
   cook Rajasthali meal with a local family (book via homestays).
5. Sustainable Tourism: Avoid plastic, support artisan cooperatives (Rajasthan
   Heritage Foundation certified shops), and respect cultural sites.
